Community Engagement & Learning

Eagle Shack is more than just a store - it's a learning environment that benefits our entire school community.

Student Volunteer Program

Eagle Shack has provided hands-on business experience to over 30 student volunteers who rotate through different roles throughout the school year. Our volunteers learn essential skills including:

  • Inventory Management: Tracking stock levels, placing orders, organizing merchandise
  • Cashier Operations: Processing transactions, handling money, providing customer service
  • Marketing: Creating promotions, designing advertisements, managing social media
  • Financial Management: Calculating profits, analyzing sales data, managing budgets
  • Leadership: Training new volunteers, resolving issues, making business decisions
  • Event Management: Coordinating concessions, managing logistics for food stands

Special Education Partnership

Eagle Shack proudly partners with the Special Education Department at Prosper High School, led by Ms. Sakina Sitabkhan, SPED Teacher-Inclusion. This partnership provides valuable workplace experience for special education students who learn:

  • Basic customer service skills
  • Money handling and simple transactions
  • Organizing and displaying products
  • Teamwork in a retail environment
  • Workplace communication and social skills

This program has been instrumental in building confidence and developing job skills that transfer to real-world employment opportunities.

Faculty Advisor: Mr. Foster

Under the guidance of our dedicated faculty advisor, Mr. Foster, Eagle Shack has flourished as both a business and educational initiative. Mr. Foster works closely with student managers and volunteers to:

  • Provide mentorship in business operations
  • Connect classroom concepts to real-world applications
  • Support the partnership with the Special Education Department
  • Ensure the store meets its financial and educational goals
  • Help students develop professional skills for future careers
  • Coordinate with clubs and organizations for merchandise and concessions

Business Success

Through the dedication of our student volunteers and the support of the Prosper High School community, Eagle Shack is on track to achieve $45,000 in sales this academic year. This success allows us to:

  • Fund various student programs and initiatives
  • Invest in expanding our product offerings
  • Support school events and activities
  • Provide real-world business experience to more students
  • Continue offering competitive prices to the student body
  • Purchase equipment for concession services and event support

Club Partnerships

Eagle Shack collaborates with numerous clubs and organizations at Prosper High School to:

  • Provide concessions for club events and fundraisers

  • Share resources and volunteer opportunities

These partnerships strengthen our school community while providing additional revenue streams for both Eagle Shack and the partnering organizations.

About Eagle Shack

Your on-campus store supporting student initiatives and providing real-world business experience.

Eagle Shack is Prosper High School's student-run store, providing convenient access to snacks, drinks, school supplies, and merchandise at competitive prices. More than just a store, Eagle Shack serves as a hands-on learning environment where student volunteers gain valuable experience in retail management, customer service, inventory control, and financial operations.

With over 30 high school volunteers rotating through different roles, Eagle Shack gives students the opportunity to develop essential business skills while serving their school community. Our services have expanded beyond retail to include merchandise design, concession services for school events, and fundraising partnerships with various clubs and organizations.

All profits from Eagle Shack sales support various student programs and initiatives at Prosper High School, creating a self-sustaining ecosystem that benefits the entire school community while providing real-world business experience to our student volunteers.
